Section 1: The Judicial Process



The Judicial Process of Hartfelden shall adhere to the following:



Lodging Complaints:

Complainants, or the complainants counsel, shall bring their case for
approval to the Richter by submitting the correct forms in the Office of
the Richter.



Notice to Appear:

Approved cases shall move on to the next stage, where a court date is set and the defendant receives a notice to appear.

Court cases cannot be scheduled within 48 hours of a Notice to Appear being issued.



Finding Counsel:

Defendants will have a maximum period of 48 hours to find counsel. If a
defendant fails to find counsel within this time and wishes to have it, a
representative shall be appointed by the court and the case shall begin
a minimum of 24 hours after Counsel appointment.

A defendant may represent themselves and are not required to obtain counsel.



The Trial



Trials shall be comprised of the seven parts listed below.



Opening Statements and Evidence Presentation:

The prosecution will be afforded time to issue an opening statement. The
opening statement will explain the charges and present initial
evidence.

The defendant or defendantâ€™s counsel will be afforded time to issue an
opening statement, refuting the charges brought against them and
providing counter evidence.



Prosecution Witnesses:

The prosecution has the right to call (an) individual(s) to the bench in
order to hear their testimony. Witnesses must be consenting and should
be afforded appropriate notice.



Counter Interrogation:

The Defense has the right to counter interrogate the prosecution's witness(es) in a respectful manner befitting the court.



Defendant Witnesses:

The defense has the right to call (an) individual(s) to the bench in
order to hear their testimony. Witnesses must be consenting and should
be afforded appropriate notice.



Counter Interrogation:

The prosecution has the right to counter interrogate the defenses witness(es) in a respectful manner befitting the court.



Closing Statements:

The Prosecution shall give their closing statements, reaffirming their evidence and argument.

The Defense shall give their closing statements, reaffirming their stance and refuting any claims against them.



Judgement



Judgement shall be decided by the Richter within 48 hours of the end of the closing statements.

Richters must be impartial and unbiased in their judgement.

Richters must keep in mind the Penal Code when coming to a final decision.

Upon a decision of judgement, the Richter shall issue a public statement from their office outlining the outcome of the trial.

Section 2: Citizens' Rights During a Trial

 The Right to a Fair Trial

Citizens have the right to a fair and impartial trial. If the Richter
feels that they cannot oversee the trial in a fair and unbiased way,
they may recuse themselves and appoint a temporary Richter over the case
for the duration of the trial.



The Right to Representation

Citizens have the right to be represented by unbiased counsel. Citizens
are not required to receive counsel, however, and may represent
themselves in court.



The Right to be Free from Harassment

Citizens have the right to be free from harassment during a trial. While
the trial is taking place, all citizens and visitors are asked to
compose themselves in a respectful manner, regardless of their personal
feelings toward either the defendant or prosecution.



The Right to Remain Silent

Defendants should be aware that any statement made may be used against
them in the process of the trial. Defendants cannot be coerced to
testify against themselves, and have the right to remain silent.
