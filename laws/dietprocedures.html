Diet Procedures Act

by AavAPRIL 19th, 2020

Article I: Voting Procedures


i. A bill shall be brought to vote upon a motion, and a second
    ii. Statutory and Regulatory law shall be brought to vote for 48 hours, Treaty and Constitutional law shall be            brought to vote for 72

        a. Statutory law shall be defined as law enacted upon the citizenry
            that does not control the actions of a ministry, a legislative house, the Courts, or the Government
        b. Regulatory law shall be defined as law that solely controls the actions of a ministry, a legislative house,                  the Courts, or the Government
        c. Treaty law shall be defined as inter-regional law, negotiated by the Government on behalf of the Kaiser
        d. Constitutional law shall be defined as any law impacting the terms of the Constitution of Hartfelden or                  any amendments thereof. It shall further be defined as any law that the Legislature wishes to grant the                  supremacy and protection of Constitutional law

iii. A bill shall pass the Reichstag upon a vote reaching the assigned threshold:

    a. A simple majority for Regulatory and Statutory legislation (50% + 1)
    b. A supermajority for Treaty and Constitutional legislation (66% + 1)

iv. Should a bill pass the Reichstag, it shall be incumbent upon the Praesident of that chamber to deliver the             legislation to the Reichssenat

    a. This shall occur within 48 hours of the passage of the bill, not including any time that the Reichssenat is                 not in session

Article II: Conduct of the Chamber

i. Members of the Reichstag are expected to conduct themselves in a respectable manner whilst engaged in             their capacity as a member of the Chamber
    a. Members of the Reichstag are discouraged from the following actions whilst engaging in legislative duties:
            i. Swearing
            ii. Cursing
            iii. Referencing content of a sexual nature
            iv. Behaving in a disrespectful or rude manner towards other members
            v. Behaving in a manner that would disgrace the Chamber
     b. Members of the Reichstag are encouraged to do the following whilst engaging in legislative duties:
        i. Offer constructive criticism
        ii. Propose legislation
        iii. Offer contradictory opinions and viewpoints
        iv. Act respectfully
        v. Use proper grammar and spelling in all matters of Legislation
        vi. Behaving in a manner that graces the Chamber
ii. Members of the Reichstag are expected to actively engage in the Chamber
    a. Should a member wish to not participate in the Reichstag, it is encouraged that they contact the                           Praesident to discover alternate means of engagement in the community
    b. Should a member fail to do so, they may find themselves being contacted by the Praesident enquiring as to          why they are not engaging
iii. Members of the Reichstag are expected to abide by all regional laws and Administrative Policies created by           the Moderation and Administration teams

Article III: Disciplinary Proceedings

i. When a member is found to be in violation of Article II of these procedures and regulations, the Praesident, at      their discretion, may initiate disciplinary proceedings against the member
        a. Such proceedings shall be entitled “Contempt of the Reichstag”
ii. A member of the Reichstag may be held in Contempt of the Reichstag under the following conditions:
    a. An order from the Praesident
    b. Initiation by a simple majority vote of the chamber
    c. A court order from a sitting Justice due to an ongoing criminal case
iii. The punishment for Contempt of Reichstag shall be limited in scope
    a. The Praesident, after a member has been found in contempt shall issue one of the following punishments:
        i. Public Censure
        ii. Removal of privilege as a member
        iii. Removal from Committee
        iv. Referral to the Office of the Crown Attorney-General for prosecution

    b. The Reichstag, may, at their discretion, overrule the punishment decided upon by the Praesident by a                    simple majority vote (50% + 1)
iv. Contempt of Reichstag shall be considered to be a punishment only issued if other diversions have not                   succeeded. The Praesident shall make all other attempts to handle the problematic member before issuing a       punishment of contempt
v. The Praesident shall be granted the power of mitigation of punishment, wherein they may reduce or suspend      punishment for a member

Article IV: Praesidental Elections

i. The Praesident shall be elected for a term until resignation or impeachment
ii. Should the Praesident resign, the Electoral Commission shall
call a snap election of all members of the Reichstag. The election shall
follow the procedure that they create

Article V: Powers of the Praesident

i. The Praesident shall be granted the following powers over legislation:
    a. The power to amend any bill or passed law for the purposes of grammatical or spelling corrections
    b. The power to organize legislation as they see fit
    c. The power to create an organizational system for legislation
ii. The Praesident shall be granted the following powers over procedure:
    a. The power to amend these standing procedures and regulations
    b. The power to create, edit, and destroy further chamber procedure and regulation
    c. The power to deliver a final ruling on chamber procedure, subservient to the Courts
iii. The Praesident shall be granted the following powers over the chamber:
    a. The power to enforce decorum
    b. The power to bring legislation to vote
    c. The power to close voting
    d. The power to specify the start and end times for voting
iv. The Praesident shall be granted the following powers over organization:
    a. The power to create, edit, and destroy committees
    b. The power to assign and remove members of the Reichstag from committees
    c. The power to designate and undesignate deputies of the Praesident
    d. The power to assign and remove duties from designated deputies
v. The Praesident shall be granted the following powers over discipline:
    a. The power to impose punishments as defined in Article III: Disciplinary Proceedings
    b. The power to mitigate punishments, as defined in Article III: Disciplinary Proceedings


Article VI: Alterspraesident

i. There shall be an Alterspraesident of the Reichstag
    a. The Alterspraesident shall be the longest serving legislative member of Hartfelden
    b. Upon the creation of these procedures, the Alterspraesident shall be Aynia
ii. The Alterspraesident shall enforce decorum in the Reichstag, as defined in Article III: Disciplinary                          Proceedings
iii. The Alterspraesident shall become Acting Praesident under the following conditions:
    a. The disappearance of the Praesident
        i. Upon the return of the Praesident, the Alterspraesident shall no longer be the Acting Praesident
    b. The resignation of the Praesident
    c. The impeachment of the Praesident
    d. The designation of “Acting Praesident” by the sitting Praesident
        i. This designation may be removed at any time by the sitting Praesident
iv. In the case of impeachment of the Praesident, the Alterspraesident shall preside

Article VII: Impeachment

i. There shall exist a mechanism for removal of an official from office
    a. This mechanism is referred to as impeachment
ii. Wherein this mechanism is prior defined in the Constitution of Hartfelden, this article seeks to grant clarity         to the actions taken during an impeachment proceeding
iii. The Reichstag shall hold the sole power of impeachment of the Reichssenat
    a. This shall be defined as the bringing of articles of impeachment, and then the trial of the Senatoren
iv. Procedure for impeachment shall be left to detailed further legislation, however:
    a. Impeachment shall be presided over by the Praesident
        i. Excepting in cases wherein the Praesident is the object of impeachment
    b. Impeachment shall require a supermajority of votes to convict (66% + 1)
    c. Impeachment shall be considered the ultimate remedy to a problem
    d. Impeachment shall not necessarily mean removal from office, the trial shall decide the fate of the accused            member
v. Upon the impeachment and successful conviction of a Senatoren, the presiding officer of the Senat shall be          notified to carry out the appropriate action, be it expulsion or censure, or any other punishment so                        designated

Article VIII: Mode of Amendment and Enactment

i. These procedures shall be amendable in the following methods:
    a. A passage of an amendment by the Reichstag with a simple majority (50% + 1)
    b. Amendment via order of the Praesident
    c. Amendment via Imperial Decree of the Kaiser
ii. Upon enactment, all prior procedures are declared null and void, their rules no longer bind this chamber
iii. Upon enactment, the sitting Praesident shall be Their Imperial Highness, Aav Ritter von Hartfelden
iv. Upon enactment, the sitting Alterspraesident shall be The Honorable Aynia von Funkeln
