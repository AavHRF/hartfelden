Electoral Procedures Act

by FriedrichMARCH 30th, 2020

Section I. Senators

Article 1. Number of Senators
The number of Senators as elected by the Reichstag shall be that of three.

Section II. Elections

Article 1. The Reichssenat shall be elected on terms of every three months.

Article 2. The Reichssenat shall be elected by one thirds of the Senat at a time

    Article 2a. The Senat shall first be elected together for three months

    Article 2b. The Senat shall serve a full term then the First Seat shall be elected.

    Article 2c. The Senat shall serve a full term and a month then the Second Seat shall be elected

    Article 2d. The Senat shall server a fuller term and two months then the Third Seat shall be                                 elected

    Article 2e. This procedures shall only occur for the first election cycle

Article 3. The Senat shall be elected using the Single Transferable Vote Method of voting

    Article 3a. Members of the Reichstag shall rank their vote as they see fit whereas each rank                                 only contains a single vote

    Article 3b. All first votes shall be counted first where those who achieve a thirty-three percentage of votes are automatically counted as winner of a seat

Article 3c. Whereas a candidate has more than thirty-three percent of the vote they shall no longer have first votes counted but instead the second votes shall be counted and so forth until all votes are counted.

Article 3d. In the case where not all seats are filled then the candidate with the least amount of votes is eliminated from the running and all those who had voted for them have their votes counted as the next in rank

Article 3e. This process shall continue until there are three candidates remaining

Article 4. The winners of the vote shall be given seats in order of which they won.

Section II. Kanzler

Article 1. The Kanzler shall be elected on a term of 90 days as defined by the Constitution

Article 2. The Kanzler shall be elected using the Alternative Vote method

Article 2a. Members of the Reichstag shall rank their vote as they see fit whereas each rank only contains a single vote

Article 2b. The First Rank votes shall be counted first and the candidate with over fifty percent of votes shall win

Article 2c. If there is not a candidate with over fifty percent then the
candidate with the lowest percentage is eliminated from the race and
all of those who had voted for them shall have their second rank votes
counted

Article 2d. This process shall continue until one candidate has over fifty percent of the Reichstag's votes

Section III. Election Commission

Article 1. The Election Commission shall be composed of three members of the Reichstag that shall be appointed by the Kaiser

Article 2. The Election Commission shall hold elections for a length of twenty four hours of voting

Article 3. The Election Commission shall open nominations where any
member of the Reichstag may stand to run for the Senat and therefore be
nominated

Article 4. The Election Commission shall count the votes as explained within Section II

Article 5. The Election Commission shall run elections when there is a
vacancy for a Senat Seat and the new Senator shall hold that seat until
the next scheduled election for that Seat
