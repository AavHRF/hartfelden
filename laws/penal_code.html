Penal Code of Hartfelden

by AavApril 20th, 2020

Section A: Definitions


1 EHC § 1

Aiding and Abetting:

Aiding and Abetting shall be defined as the offense of helping another
commit a crime, or through inaction, allowing a crime to be committed.

1 EHC § 2

Attempt to Commit

Attempt to Commit shall be defined as the offense of attempting to commit any crime listed in this document.

1 EHC § 3

Conspiracy to Commit (Misdemeanor)

Conspiracy to Commit (Misdemeanor) shall be defined as the offense of
planning to commit a crime at the misdemeanor level under this act.

1 EHC § 4

Conspiracy to Commit (Felony)

Conspiracy to Commit (Felony) shall be defined as the offense of
planning to commit a crime at the felony level under this act. It shall
be alternatively be defined as the offense of planning with multiple
persons to commit any crime under this act.

1 EHC § 5

Treason

Treason shall be defined as the offense of committing an act to
overthrow the government of Hartfelden, or to levy war against
Hartfelden, or to adhere to, or give aid and comfort, to the enemies of
Hartfelden.

1 EHC § 6

Sabotage

Sabotage shall be defined as the offense of committing an act that would
cause damage to a communication platform of the region, or an act made
with the intent to damage the foreign or military policy of the region.

1 EHC § 7

Espionage

Espionage shall be defined as the offense of procuring sensitive or
classified information for nefarious purposes, or sharing sensitive or
classified information with a person who is not cleared for such
information

1 EHC § 8

Corruption

Corruption shall be defined as misusing a public office for personal gain, or for the gain of others.

1 EHC § 9

Impersonation

Impersonation shall be defined as the offense of pretending to be another person for any official purpose.

1 EHC § 10

Impersonation of a Public Official

Impersonation of a Public Official shall be defined as general Impersonation, except for it being a Public Official

1 EHC § 11

Trolling

Trolling shall be defined as the offense of making a post or sending a message with the intent of angering another person.

1 EHC § 12

Blackmail

Blackmail shall be defined as the offense of using personal information
in an attempt to get a result from the person who is being blackmailed.

1 EHC § 13

Perjury

Perjury shall be defined as the offense of willfully telling an untruth in a court after having taken an oath or affirmation.

1 EHC § 14

Failure to Comply with a Court Order

Failure to Comply with a Court Order shall be defined as the offense of not following a lawfully issued order by a court of law.

1 EHC § 15

Contempt of Court

Contempt of Court shall be defined as acting in a manner whilst in a
court of law that is disobedient to or disrespectful of a court of law
and its officers.

Section B: Felonious punishments and Misdemeanor Punishments


i. A crime shall be considered a felony if

a. It is a repeat offense (committed more than three times)

and/or
b. It is one these crimes listed below

    i. Treason
    ii. Sabotage
    iii. Espionage
    iv. Corruption
    v. Impersonation of a Public Official
ii. A crime shall be considered a misdemeanor if
a. It is committed three or fewer times

and
b. It is a crime other than the crimes listed above for a felony
The punishment for a felony shall be no less than a one day ban, and shall not be more than a one year ban, excepting in treason cases which may be considered for a permanent ban.

The punishment for a misdemeanor shall have no minimum, but shall not exceed a three day ban.
